# Bloomli Brand + Product Context for Synthetic Demo Reviews

Use this context whenever generating, cleaning, or auditing Bloomli demo reviews.

**Important:** These reviews are synthetic demo content for mockups, app testing, Shopify theme previews, and client-facing demo stores. They must never be presented as real customer testimonials on a live storefront.

---

## Brand Overview

Bloomli is a women's hair wellness brand focused on making hair-support routines feel simple, beautiful, and realistic. The brand sits between science-backed supplement brands and warm beauty/wellness brands. The voice should feel like a smart friend with a science background: clear, warm, grounded, and not overly clinical.

Bloomli is not a miracle-cure brand. It should not sound medical, exaggerated, shame-based, or fake. The review language should feel casual and believable, like real customers writing in a review widget.

---

## Brand Voice for Reviews

Reviews should sound:
- Real and human
- Casual, not polished
- Sometimes short and simple
- Sometimes slightly specific
- Occasionally imperfect in phrasing
- Mostly positive, but not all perfect
- Focused on routine, taste, consistency, packaging, texture, ease of use, and how the product fits into daily life

Reviews should not sound:
- Like ads
- Like product-page copy
- Like AI wrote them
- Too long across every row
- Overly emotional or dramatic
- Medical or disease-focused
- Like every person saw the exact same result
- Like every person mentions every ingredient

Use a natural mix:
- Very short reviews: "Tastes good," "Love the taste," "Easy to remember," "Part of my morning now."
- Short casual reviews: 1 sentence
- Medium reviews: 2 to 3 sentences
- Longer reviews: only occasionally
- Some reviews can be about shipping, packaging, taste, texture, habit-building, or not noticing much yet

---

## Core Compliance Rules

Do not use claims like:
- regrow hair
- stop hair loss
- cure hair loss
- reverse thinning
- fixed my bald spots
- guaranteed results
- miracle
- clinically proven
- FDA-approved
- works like minoxidil
- cured alopecia
- no side effects
- changed my life overnight

Use softer, realistic wording:
- "my hair feels healthier"
- "my routine feels easier"
- "I've been more consistent"
- "my nails feel stronger"
- "my hair looks shinier"
- "I noticed less visible shedding over time"
- "I'm starting to see a difference"
- "too early to tell, but I like taking them"
- "not sure yet, but the taste is good"
- "easy to keep up with"

Avoid "before and after" style language. Do not write dramatic transformation reviews.

## Review Length and Style Rules

For every large review batch:
- Include short one-line reviews
- Include casual medium reviews
- Include a few longer reviews
- Include some neutral or slightly negative reviews
- Do not make every review mention results
- Do not make every review mention ingredients
- Do not make every review sound like a marketing sentence
- Do not repeat title structures too much
- Do not repeat the same opening phrases too often

**Good short bodies:**
- "Tastes good and easy to take."
- "Love the daily ritual."
- "So far so good."
- "A little sweet, but I like them."
- "Easy to remember."
- "Nice texture."
- "Not much to report yet, but I like the flavor."

**Bad body style:**
- "This revolutionary formula transformed my hair and gave me the confidence I always dreamed of."
- "Bloomli cured my hair loss in only weeks."
- "The scientifically advanced ingredients made my follicles regenerate."
- "I will never use anything else ever again."

## Rating Distribution Guidance

For a realistic high-rated demo file:
- Many 5-star reviews
- Some 4-star reviews with small critiques
- Some 3-star reviews that are neutral or "too early to tell"
- A few 2-star reviews with taste, shipping, texture, or expectation issues
- A very small number of 1-star reviews (usually taste, delivery, damaged bottle, or no results)

Avoid making low-star reviews sound fake or too harsh. Do not include safety scare claims, disease claims, or extreme allegations.

## Title Guidance

**Good titles:** "Easy to take," "Love the taste," "Part of my morning," "So far so good," "Actually consistent," "Good little routine," "Nice flavor," "Better than pills," "Too early but promising," "A little sweet," "Works for my routine"

**Avoid repeating patterns:** "Great gummies for me," "Great product for me," "Great taste for me," "Great routine for me"

**Avoid overclaiming titles:** "Hair regrowth miracle," "Stopped my hair loss," "Fixed my thinning," "Clinically proven results"

---

## Similarity and Uniqueness Rules

When generating large batches:
- Every title must be unique
- Every body must be unique
- Avoid near-duplicate titles (threshold: < 0.90 similarity)
- Avoid near-duplicate bodies (threshold: < 0.85 similarity)
- No exact duplicate reviewer names
- No exact duplicate emails
- No em dashes
- Vary sentence length, structure, topic, and tone
- Use different customer situations
- Use different levels of enthusiasm

---

## Reviewer Names

Use realistic full names with first and last name. Mix common American names naturally. Avoid celebrity names, joke names, obviously fake names, or repeating names.

---

## Final Rule

Every review should answer this question:
> "Could a real adult customer have written this after trying this exact product?"

If the answer is no, rewrite it.
